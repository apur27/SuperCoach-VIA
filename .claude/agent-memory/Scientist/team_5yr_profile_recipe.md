The README's "Team playing styles — 5 years of data" section is auto-generated by `generate_5year_profiles()` in `update_team_analysis.py`.

Window: the five seasons immediately prior to the current detected year (e.g. 2026 run → 2021..2025). Auto-rolls.

**Why:** stat fingerprints from a 5-year aggregate are far more stable than any single season and reveal coaching DNA rather than form. A new section appears via markers `<!-- 5YEAR-TEAM-PROFILES-START -->` / `<!-- 5YEAR-TEAM-PROFILES-END -->`, inserted after the current-year analysis section.

**How to apply:**
- Numerical methodology: sum player stats to team-game first → mean across that team's games per year → simple mean of yearly means (not pooled). Trends are linear-fit slopes converted to relative change `slope * (year_max - year_min) / mean`.
- Profile ranks are recomputed from scratch over the 18 teams in the window. Lower-is-better ranks (clangers, free_kicks_against) use rank 1 = lowest raw value.
- The "vulnerability" framing in the closing sentence distinguishes `GENUINE_WEAKNESS_STATS` (low = bad) from stylistic-low stats (handballs, uncontested possessions, rebound_50s, hit_outs) — the latter are framed as deliberate trade-offs rather than flaws.
- Re-running is idempotent: same input produces byte-identical README between markers.
- The section regenerates as part of `./refresh_and_rank.sh` because that script already calls `update_team_analysis.py`.

If you change the window to extend before 2012 the script will hit teams with `seasons < 5` and emit a "(note: N/5 seasons of data — partial coverage)" tag. GWS, Gold Coast, Brisbane Lions are all complete from 2021.
