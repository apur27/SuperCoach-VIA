**Recurring tension:** When a team wins (or loses) materially with two or more key personnel absent, the Structuralist + Conditioner lenses tend to read the result as evidence of system depth, while the Match-up Tactician + List Strategist read it as a single-game artefact flattered by opposition weakness.

**Why:** The user has framed at least one post-match analysis (Adelaide vs Richmond R9 2026) where this exact split appeared - Adelaide won without Walker (KPF) and without Berry firing, against a Richmond side missing their #1 ruck. The system-depth claim is multi-game by definition; the evidence offered is single-game.

**How to apply:** When the user presents a single-match finding that invites a "system over talent" or "talent over system" claim, default to Contested or Probationary tier and require a multi-game tripwire (e.g., "repeat the performance against a top-six opponent with first-choice ruck") before any tier upgrade. Do not let the Structuralist's read swamp the others on n=1 evidence.

**2026-05-11 reinforcement:** Adelaide vs Richmond R9 2026 post-mortem played to this split exactly. The "Berry single-point-of-failure" thesis was inverted (Berry 3 tackles, Adelaide team tackle count went UP, not down - 161% of season rate). The post-mortem correctly framed Adelaide's pressure system as distributed-not-Berry-dependent, but also flagged that this is n=1 evidence and the right falsification test is a 5-game sample of low-Berry games. **Pattern to repeat:** when a "single point of failure" thesis fails on one game, explicitly call out the null-hypothesis test (does the team metric hold up across multiple games where the named player has a flat day?) before declaring the thesis dead. One game is suggestive; five is decisive.

**Forward-supply-chain corollary surfaced same game:** Richmond's "Ryan as the single structural lever" thesis also failed on Ryan-out (the brief had no Plan B written). This is the same structural pattern in reverse - a one-player structural-lever claim that lacked a contingency branch. When the user proposes "Player X is the structural lever, win/lose hinges on his availability" the council should ask for the explicit Plan B in the brief, and tier-cap the recommendation at Probationary if no Plan B is documented.
