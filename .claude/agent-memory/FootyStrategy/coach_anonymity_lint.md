name: Coach anonymity rule - common slip points in published docs
description: Where the "never name coaches" rule tends to leak in this project's articles, and how to anonymise without losing the structural lesson

---

The "never name the coaches" anonymity rule (council hard rule) tends to leak into published articles at three predictable spots, even when the rest of the piece is disciplined:

1. **Historical parallels** — e.g. invoking the Melbourne 2012-21 rebuild and naming Neeld / Roos / Goodwin by name to make the structural point. The fix is functional: "the 2012-13 stretch", "the appointment framed as stabiliser, not saviour", "the senior coach who eventually won the 2021 flag". The lesson survives; the names go.

2. **Opposition coach as a stand-in for opposition system** — e.g. "Ross Lyon typically asks his half-back line to...", "any brief for a Matthew Nicks side must...". Replace with the team identity: "St Kilda's defensive identity asks...", "any brief for an Adelaide opponent must...". The coach is the team's structural fingerprint, not the source of authority.

3. **Hypothetical "what should X coach do" framing** — e.g. "the question for Damien Hardwick / Mark Williams is...". Replace with "the question for Richmond's coaching staff is...". Same content, no name attribution.

**Why:** the council's authority is borrowed from a tradition; naming current or historical coaches turns the piece into appeal-to-personality rather than structural argument. The rule also protects the project from looking like it's putting words in named people's mouths.

**How to apply:** when reviewing a published doc, grep for the working list of AFL coaches likely to appear (current senior coaches across all 18 clubs, plus recent retirees like Hardwick, Buckley, Bolton, Roos, Neeld, Goodwin, Voss-as-coach). When the name is the subject of the article itself (e.g. "Voss steps down" piece), the headline subject can be named; the council's structural reads inside the article cannot.

**Exception:** Codex-attributed external commentary sections (e.g. "Codex's specific candidates: ...") may name names because they are explicitly attributed to an external source, not the council. The FootyStrategy framework around such sections (Tier A/B/C rankings, mistakes-to-avoid) still cannot pick or endorse a specific name.

**Player names are fine.** The rule is coach-specific. Players are routinely named in tactical analysis (Dawson, Milera, Cripps, Walsh, etc.) — this is normal football vocabulary.
