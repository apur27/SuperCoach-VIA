## What this measures

A goal assist is the disposal immediately preceding a teammate's goal - the kick or handball that creates the shot. It is the most direct quantification of "playmaking" available in the data. Unlike the [goals page](hall-of-fame-stat-goals.md), which measures the finisher, this list measures the creator: the player whose vision and disposal skill produces the scoring chance.

> **Data caveat.** Goal assists have only been reliably tracked from approximately **1999**. Pre-1999 goal assists are not in the database. This is functionally a post-2000 leaderboard. The great pre-2000 playmakers (Carey, Watson, Buckley pre-2000, Bartlett, Mark Williams, Skilton) are absent because the stat did not exist.

## FootyStrategy tactical read

**Pendlebury and the modern playmaker template.** Scott Pendlebury 328 goal assists **[data]** is the all-time leader. Eddie Betts 318 **[data]** sits second, only seven assists back, on a vastly higher per-game rate (0.91 to 0.75) **[data]**. *Structuralist lens:* Pendlebury and Betts represent two different paths to the same destination. Pendlebury is the midfielder-distributor whose assists come from clearance-to-forward delivery and from secondary use after marks 50-60 metres out. Betts is the small forward whose assists come from inside-50 close-range craft - the no-look handball, the short snap-pass, the cutting kick from inside the goal square that he saw before anyone else. The fact that they finish 1-2 on the list shows the assist as a stat is genuinely role-agnostic.

**Robbie Gray's per-game rate is the highest.** Robbie Gray 262 assists in 271 games at 0.97/game **[data]** is the highest sustained per-game rate in the top 20 and one of the only careers above 0.90. *Match-up Tactician lens:* Gray's Port Adelaide role across his prime years was specifically as the half-forward who turned the back-of-pack possession into a forward 50 entry that hit a target. Almost every Gray possession inside attacking half ended in a forward-50 disposal; the assists accumulated naturally from a role design that prioritised final-third creation over personal goal-scoring.

**The key-forward assist contribution.** Hawkins 296 **[data]**, J. Riewoldt 249 **[data]**, Walker 243 **[data]**, Franklin 231 **[data]**, N. Riewoldt 224 **[data]** - five key forwards in the top 16. *Tempo Architect lens:* the modern key forward is also expected to be a distributor - the high mark on the wing, the handball-out from a marking contest, the centring kick from the boundary line. Hawkins's 296 is particularly notable: his forward-fifty work as a marking target produced both his own goals (796 **[data]** career, see [goals page](hall-of-fame-stat-goals.md)) and roughly one assist every fourth game. The role evolution from "finisher only" to "finisher and creator" is one of the most measurable shifts of the post-2010 forward-line.

**Bontempelli's projected trajectory.** Bontempelli 239 assists in 273 games at 0.88/game **[data]** is the highest per-game rate among players still in their twenties. *Talent Developer lens:* if he plays out another 80-100 games at his current rate, his career total will clear 350 and overhaul Pendlebury's mark. His role - the centre-bounce midfielder who pushes forward in the inside-50 and either takes the mark or finds the open teammate - is built around the assist outcome. The next decade's leaderboard will likely have Bontempelli and Daicos at #1 and #2.

**Why the assist stat lags the rest of football analytics.** Goal assists were not officially tracked by the AFL until 1999, several years after disposals, marks, and tackles were standardised. *Conditioner lens:* the lag is itself instructive - the league moved to track team-impact stats (clearances, contested possessions, inside-50s) before it tracked individual creativity stats (assists, score involvements, score launches). The data-richness of the modern game lets us see now what we could not see 30 years ago: the highest-impact creators are not always the highest possession-getters, and the player who sets up the goal often deserves as much credit as the player who kicks it.

## Data coverage

- **Goal assists tracked from 1999.** Pre-1999 numbers are not in the database.
- **Definition stable since recording began** - no major methodology change.
- **The "score involvement" stat** (a broader version that counts any disposal in the chain ending in a score) is not in this database.

## Methodology

Sum of per-game `goal_assists` field across each player's career (post-1999); per-game rate is total ÷ games_played in the post-1999 window.
