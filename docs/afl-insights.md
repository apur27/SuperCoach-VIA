## Round 20 — Week in Review

**Disposal leaders (rounds 1–19):** Nick Daicos (Collingwood) tops the competition at **34.8 per game** **[data]**, ahead of Bailey Smith (Geelong) at **32.2** **[data]** and Clayton Oliver (Greater Western Sydney) at **31.9** **[data]** — all well clear of the league mean of **15.08 per game** **[data]**.

**Ladder:** Fremantle have stretched their lead after Round 19 to **14-2** (56 points, 143.7%) **[data]**, now **eight** **[data]** competition points clear of 2nd-placed Sydney (**12-3**, 48 points) **[data]** — the round's clearest ladder movement, and the largest top-two gap of the season to date.

**Watch in Round 20:** Harry Sheezel (North Melbourne) is the model's top projected disposal getter at **30.0** **[data]**, just ahead of Nasiah Wanganeen-Milera (St Kilda) and Finn Callaghan (GWS), both projected at **29.0** **[data]**.

**Tactical note:** Contested possessions remain the clearest predictor of clearance output (r = **+0.74** **[data]**); Clayton Oliver (GWS) leads both — **15.53** contested possessions per game **[data]** and **7.94** clearances per game **[data]** — the dataset's cleanest evidence that stoppage dominance and midfield disposal volume are the same skill expressed twice.

*Methodology: disposal-leader figures, the league mean, and the contested-possessions/clearances correlation are read from `docs/afl-stat-leaders-2026.md` (rounds 1–19 per-game aggregates over `data/player_data/`, auto-generated by `update_team_analysis.py`). Ladder figures — W-L record, points, percentage, and the top-two points gap — are read from the Round 19 ladder in `docs/afl-finals-2026.md`, itself derived from `data/matches/matches_2026.csv`. Round 20 disposal projections are read from `data/prediction/next_round_20_prediction_20260713_2050.csv`.*
