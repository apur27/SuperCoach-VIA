## What this measures

The career leaderboards on the other [stat-leaders sub-pages](hall-of-fame-stat-leaders.md) reward longevity multiplied by per-game rate. This page does the opposite: it isolates the single-best season any player has ever produced in each category. A single-season record is a snapshot of physical and tactical peak - the largest 22-26 game stretch of dominance a player can sustain inside one football year. The records also act as a tripwire for rule changes, recording-methodology shifts, and structural moves in the way the game is played. When a single-season record falls or stops being approached, something underneath the sport has moved.

## Reading across the records

Three patterns connect every table on this page.

**The pre-1990 era is preserved only in the goals and kicks records.** Hudson, Pratt, Bartlett, Wayne Richardson, Ross Smith, Mike Green, Greg Parke, Dempsey - the pre-1990 names cluster in the goals, kicks, and marks tables. Every other category is dominated by post-2000 entries because the underlying stat was either not tracked or was tracked under a different methodology. *Tempo Architect lens:* this is not a statement about player quality. It is a measurement of which actions the league chose to count and when it started counting them.

**The modern records all sit in two narrow windows.** The handballs, contested possessions, disposals, and inside-50s peaks all cluster between 2016 and 2024. The hit-outs peaks all sit in 2015-2019. The tackles peaks have crept upward through 2024-2025. *Structuralist lens:* this is rule design at work. The 2014 interchange cap pushed midfielder rotation patterns; the 2017 hit-out methodology change reset the ruck recording standard; the 2019 stand-the-mark rule shifted possession-retention patterns. Each rule change is followed within 2-3 years by a new ceiling in the affected stat category. The records on this page double as a timeline of how the league has evolved.

**The Pendlebury absence is informative.** Of the players who appear in the [career leaderboards](hall-of-fame-stat-leaders.md), Pendlebury - #1 in career disposals, tackles, handballs, and goal assists - appears in *zero* single-season top-10 lists on this page. *Talent Developer lens:* his career is built on consistency-over-density rather than peak-output. His best disposal season was 700, his best tackle season 110, his best handball season ~290. He achieved the all-time totals not by ever leading the league in a single year, but by sustaining a top-quartile rate across 21 seasons without injury interruption. The single-season records and the career records measure two genuinely different things, and the same player rarely tops both.

## Data coverage

- **Goals** reliable for full VFL/AFL history; pre-1968 the season was 18 home-and-away games, post-1994 it is 22-23.
- **Kicks and marks** reliable from approximately 1965 league-wide.
- **Handballs** under-recorded pre-1965; the single-season list is effectively post-1965.
- **Tackles** league-wide from 1987.
- **Contested possessions, clearances, inside-50s, goal assists** league-wide from 1998-1999.
- **Hit-outs** changed recording methodology in 2017; pre-2017 totals understate slightly.
- **Brownlow votes** stored on the modern 3-2-1 scale (in use since 1981); pre-1981 5-4-3-2-1 votes rescaled.
