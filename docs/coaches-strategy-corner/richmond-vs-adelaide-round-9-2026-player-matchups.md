## The five matchups that decide the game

### 1. Jack Ross on Sam Berry — the contested-ball pivot

| | Ross | Berry |
|---|---:|---:|
| Games 2026 | 8 | 8 |
| Disposals/g | 23.2 | 22.5 |
| Contested poss/g | 11.9 | **13.4** |
| Tackles/g | 5.1 | **8.0** |
| Clearances/g | 5.1 | 5.1 |
| Disposal CV | 0.19 | 0.19 |
| Trend | **+0.83** | +0.62 |

**Read**: This is the single most important matchup in the game. Berry is Adelaide's tackle leader AND contested-poss leader — remove him from the contest and Adelaide's pressure profile goes from 4/18 to mid-pack. Ross is Richmond's clearance leader (5.1/g) and his disposal trend is positive. He has the engine to follow Berry into all three phases.

**Plan**: Body Berry first — accept he wins the first contest, beat him to the second. Berry is durable but has *never* in 2026 had to chase a runner with Ross's spread. Ross can sit on him at centre bounces, then get to the next stoppage as the receive.

**Trigger to abandon**: If Berry has 7+ CPs and 4+ tackles by half-time, swap the assignment to Taranto and free Ross for inside-50 receives.

### 2. Tim Taranto on Jordan Dawson — captain on senior

| | Taranto | Dawson |
|---|---:|---:|
| Games 2026 | 6 | 5 |
| Disposals/g | 22.3 | **24.6** |
| Contested poss/g | **12.0** | 8.4 |
| Tackles/g | **6.2** | 4.6 |
| Clearances/g | 5.8 | 4.5 |
| Goals/g | 0.50 | 1.20 |
| Disposal CV | 0.30 | **0.11** |
| Trend | **–2.91** | +0.95 |

**Read**: Dawson is the most consistent ball-winner in either squad (CV 0.11). His range is 21–28 — he gives you exactly what he gives you. Taranto is the ceiling on contested ball but trending down sharply.

**Plan**: Senior on senior. Taranto's contested-ball ceiling matches Dawson's volume. The battle isn't about Taranto winning more disposals — it's about denying Dawson the time to launch from the wing. Dawson's 1.20 g/g (6 goals in 5 games) comes from the same arc as Jordan Dawson the long-kicking 2024 vintage; tight man defence with the wings closed.

**Trigger to abandon**: If Dawson hits 18 disposals at half-time, Taranto's role changes to a stoppage-only matchup and Hopper (defensive midfielder) takes the open-play role.

### 3. Nick Vlastuin on Taylor Walker — body on the spearhead

| | Vlastuin | Walker |

| Games 2026 | 7 | 7 |
| Goals/g (per game played) | 0.14 | **1.57** |
| Goals total | 1 | 11 |
| Goals max in a game | — | 5 |
| Disposals/g | 14.3 | 11.2 |
| Marks/g | 4.3 | 5.2 |
| Marks inside 50/g | — | 2.2 |
| Trend | +0.54 | –1.72 |

**Read**: Walker is the goalkicker. 11 goals from 7 games (1.57 g/g), max-game 5. He is the player most likely to win the game by himself. Vlastuin is the body for the contest — experienced, slow-of-foot but heavy. Walker, late-career, is also slow.

**Plan**: Front and square. Vlastuin in front of Walker for every centring kick; Walker behind his man on every long bomb. Walker is no longer a leading target — he is a stationary contest in the goal square. Force him outside the 50m arc; outside the arc he kicks 30% set shots historically. Inside, 60%+.

**Trigger to abandon**: If Walker has 2 goals at half-time, double up — Tom Brown to the next contest.

### 4. Samson Ryan vs Thilthorpe / Maley — the structural lever

| | Ryan (Richmond) | Thilthorpe (Ade fwd/r) | Maley (Ade fwd/r) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Games 2026 | 3 | 8 | 5 |
| Hit-outs/g | **16.3** | 3.3 | 7.0 |
| Combined Adelaide HO/g | — | **8.8 (18/18)** | — |
| Marks/g | — | 5.4 | 2.0 |
| Goals/g | — | 1.62 | 1.00 |
| Marks inside 50/g | — | high | 2.0 |

**Read**: Adelaide does not have a recognised AFL ruckman in this rotation. Thilthorpe is a forward who gives 3 minutes a quarter at the contest; Maley is a depth tall doing the same. Their combined hit-out total is 8.8/g — *the single worst in the league by 24 hit-outs to the league average* **[data]**. Ryan, when he plays, averages 16.3 hit-outs across just 3 games this year.

**Plan**: This is not a matchup, it is a mismatch. Ryan to dominate centre bounces, throw-ins, and forward 50 stoppages. The downstream effect is that Thilthorpe spends more minutes at the contest and fewer minutes in the goal square — taking him out of his marks-and-goals role. **One stat to win this game: hit-outs +15 or better.**

**Trigger to abandon**: there isn't one. If Ryan gets through his 70% game time, this lever holds for the full game.

**Read**: Two distributors out of half-back, almost identical disposal averages. Whichever distributor gets to launch into open ground sets the territory of the game.

Milera is the equivalent for Adelaide; pressure his receive — Hugo Ralphsmith (5 games, 14.2 d/g, +0.31 trend) is the natural foil who can run with him.

**Trigger to abandon**: If Short's disposal count is below 12 at half-time, the game has been pushed too defensive — release him forward of the wing.

## Adelaide threats — full list and how to neutralise them

Ranked by 2026 game-impact (composite of disposal, contested ball, and goal contribution).

### Sam Berry — midfielder/tackler

22.5 disp/g, 13.4 CP/g, 8.0 tk/g, 5.1 cl/g, +0.62 trend, CV 0.19.

He is the engine. Tackle-and-CP leader; the system reduces if he is removed.

Plan: Hard tag with Ross. Restrict to under 9 CPs and 5 tackles.

### Jordan Dawson — captain, midfielder

24.6 disp/g, 8.4 CP/g, 4.6 tk/g, 4.5 cl/g, **CV 0.11 (most consistent ball-winner)**, +0.95 trend, 6 goals in 5 games (1.20 g/g).

The flag-bearer. Long left foot launching from wing. Six goals across five games is significant production from a midfielder.

Plan: Taranto senior matchup. Cut his time on the long kick. Force the handball.

### Wayne Milera — half-back distributor

25.5 disp/g, 5.8 m/g, 6.6 CP/g, +0.10 trend, CV 0.21.

Connector. Doesn't tackle (2.8/g), doesn't go to the contest. Pure ball-mover.

Plan: Pressure the receive. Hugo Ralphsmith. Don't double-team Berry by leaving Milera free.

### Josh Worrell — intercept defender

24.1 disp/g, 6.4 m/g, 6.8 CP/g, –0.99 trend, CV 0.18.

Backline pillar. Marks at 6.4/g — he is the intercept target on every Richmond inside-50.

Plan: Don't bomb to him. Short kicks across half-forward to retain. Go around, not over.

### Rory Laird — uncontested distributor

23.0 disp/g, **7.2 m/g (squad-high marks)**, 5.0 CP/g, +0.03 trend.

The veteran outside accumulator. Plays through midfield/half-back; high mark count is launching-pad accumulation.

Plan: Forward press; cut his uncontested receives in the back half. He is a chain-of-three mark accumulator — break the first link.

### Riley Thilthorpe — key forward / pinch-hit ruck

12.9 disp/g, 5.4 m/g, **13 goals (squad top), 1.62 g/g**, 6.4 CP/g, **3.3 HO/g**.

Dual-role — when he ruckings, Walker becomes the only contested forward target. When he forwards, he is the contested mark in the goal square.

Plan: When he is forward, Tom Brown senior body on him. When he goes ruck, immediate kick-out from defensive 50 to a contested mark — exploit Adelaide's depleted ruck-rotation moment.

### Taylor Walker — goalkicker

11.2 disp/g (only 7 games of contact ball), **11 goals from 7 games (1.57 g/g)**, max 5, 5.2 m/g, 2.2 marks inside 50/g, trend –1.72.

The danger man. Goal-kicker with experience and a feel for the contest in front. The 5-goal game (Round 7 vs St Kilda at home) already in 2026 means his ceiling is intact, but the trend is negative — he is not building, just finishing.

Plan: Vlastuin on him with strong front position. Don't let the inside-50 entry give him a clean lead.

### Josh Rachele — half-forward / running forward

20.8 disp/g, 12 goals from 8 games (1.50 g/g), 8.6 CP/g, 3.4 tk/g, –1.40 trend, CV 0.29.

Running half-forward. Explosive in transition. CP rank for a forward is unusually high — he is competing inside.

Plan: Match a runner. Sam Banks (19.6 d/g, +1.4 mid-game role) or Tyler Sonsie. Limit his reception time.

### Izak Rankine — small forward

17.3 disp/g, 8 goals from 7 games (1.14 g/g), 8.4 CP/g, 3.6 tk/g, **+1.82 trend**, CV 0.32.

Heating up. Trend is the second-highest in the squad. Impact player who chains transition through the corridor.

Plan: Don't lose him at stoppages. He is the snap-shooter; the best defence is forcing him to pick up the ball at half-forward, not in the goal square.

### Ben Keays — small forward / pressure

14.8 disp/g, 10 goals from 8 games (1.25 g/g), 4.2 tk/g, 0.16 CV (consistent), +0.31 trend.

The pressure-and-finish small forward. Tackle ratings are forward-50 concentrated.

Plan: Don't get caught with the ball at half-back. Keays' goals come from defensive turnovers.

### James Peatling — midfielder / tackler

20.6 disp/g, 9.9 CP/g, 4.9 tk/g, 5.0 cl/g, +0.65 trend.

Second-tier midfielder behind Dawson and Berry. Genuine third option.

Plan: Hopper or McAuliffe. His clearance threat is real (5.0/g).

### Alex Neal-Bullen — utility

17.4 disp/g, 5 goals from 7 games (0.71 g/g), 3.1 tk/g, 1.8 cl/g, –0.70 trend.

Versatile, mid-pack on most metrics.

Plan: No specific matchup; rotate the running defenders on him.

## Richmond's match-winners — how to maximise them

### Jack Ross — Richmond's Sam Berry

23.2 disp/g, 11.9 CP/g, 5.1 tk/g, 5.1 cl/g, **+0.83 trend, CV 0.19**.

The leader of the engine room. Numbers and trend say he is the most reliable senior midfielder in form right now.

Maximise: Centre bounces, hard inside role on Berry, then to the next stoppage. Don't wing-rotate him; he is a contest player.

### Tim Taranto — contested ceiling, watch the trend

22.3 disp/g, 12.0 CP/g, 6.2 tk/g, 5.8 cl/g, 3 goals in 6 games (0.50 g/g). **Trend –2.91 (concerning)**. CV 0.30.

The CP-and-tackle ceiling, but the trend says he is in a form dip.

Maximise: Define his role narrowly. Senior matchup on Dawson at stoppages, then off the field. Don't ask him to play 75% game time at this trend.

### Dion Prestia — improving veteran

21.3 disp/g, 4.0 tk/g, 2.8 cl/g, **+1.83 trend (squad-best of regulars)**, CV 0.20.

The improving senior body. Goals/g 0.1 — but his inside-50 entries (3.7/g) are quality.

Maximise: Wing-half-forward role; let him push forward as the third midfielder at stoppages. He is the late-quarter asset.

### Jacob Hopper — hard ball winner

22.0 disp/g, 11.4 CP/g, 5.4 cl/g, **CV 0.14 (most consistent in squad)**, +0.05 trend.

The most reliable disposal-floor player on the list. Doesn't have ceiling minutes but gives 22 every week.

Maximise: Defensive midfield; tag the second-tier Adelaide midfielder (Peatling). Do not move him into a free running role — the value is the floor, not the upside.

### Kane McAuliffe — emerging

20.8 disp/g across 5 games, 4.0 tk/g, 8.0 CP/g, **+1.56 trend**, 2 goals in 5 games (0.40 g/g).

Heating up. Late-quarter contributor.

Maximise: 60% game time. Late-game role at stoppages when senior bodies are tiring.

### Jayden Short — Richmond's distributor

Already covered as priority matchup. Critical to the half-back launch.

### Sam Lalor — emerging key forward / midfielder

Forward-mid hybrid. The squad's third inside-50 contributor.

Maximise: Stoppage-receive role inside 50. He is the link between the midfield and the goal-kickers.

### Seth Campbell — small-forward goal-kicker

The Walker-equivalent for Richmond at half his age. Roughly the same scoring rate per game, smaller body, much more running.

## Caveats

- All averages are 2026-only with sample sizes shown. Players with <3 games are noted but treated cautiously.
- Trend = simple linear slope. With 5–8 data points, trend is *directional indication*, not a forecast — it can be reversed by a single game.
- CV is meaningful only above ~12 disp/g and 5+ games; small samples are flagged.
- No injury or availability check applied. The team list is the team list — selection is the senior coach's call. This brief assumes the names listed are available.
- Position labels are inferred from stat profile, not authoritative. Use the senior coach's positional template; the matchup recommendations transfer directly.
