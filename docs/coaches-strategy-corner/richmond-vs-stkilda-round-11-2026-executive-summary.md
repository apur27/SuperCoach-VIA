## The 3 things that decide this game

1. **Milera's absence reshapes St Kilda's transition.** Wanganeen-Milera is the Saints' kicking spine - **26.8 disp/g and 19.5 kicks/g across 8 games, with a +1.98 disp/round trend** **[data]**. Bradley Hill is the listed replacement and has been excellent in his own right (23.3 disp, 15.8 kicks/g) **[data]** - but the gap is 3.7 kicks per game, and the Saints' rebound chain leans on the long left foot Milera provides. The wing-half-back kicking floor for St Kilda drops measurably with Hill carrying the load alone.
2. **Richmond's path is contested and territorial.** The Tigers are 18/18 in the league for disposals (330.7/g) and 18/18 for goals (9.2/g) **[data]** - the worst-scoring side in the competition. The lever is not volume; it is **clearance-into-quality-entry**. Taranto (12.9 CP/g, 6.3 cl/g), Hopper (11.1 CP/g, 5.0 cl/g) and Ross (11.7 CP/g, 4.9 cl/g) **[data]** are the engine that has to win the stoppages and convert them into Lynch/Lalor/Campbell leads. The league-worst scoring is the blocker; the only way to fix it for one afternoon is to shorten the field.
3. **The H2H is brutal but 2025 R23 was a 4-point game.** St Kilda have won **5 in a row by an average of +35.4** **[data]**. The single most-recent fixture - 2025 R23 at the MCG - was a **4-point Saints win (56-52)** **[data]**. This game is closer than the ladder (RIC 1-8, STK 4-4) and the H2H ledger say. The 35-point average is dragged by two outliers (R23 2024 -48, R4 2025 -82). The 2025 R23 tape is the real reference point.

## Richmond's edge

The only headline lever is the ruck *if Samson Ryan is fit*. Ryan averaged **16.3 hit-outs/g across his 3 games** **[data]** and Rowan Marshall is averaging 12.5 HO/g for the Saints **[data]** - a +4 hit-out swing per game is realistic, and at the centre bounce that translates to clearance counts. Contested mark count is 7/18 (9.56/g) **[data]** - one of the few volume metrics where Richmond is above the league average and above St Kilda (10/18, 8.56/g) **[data]**. Tom Lynch (1.40 g/g across 5 games, 2.6 m_i50/g) **[data]** and Sam Lalor (1.14 g/g across 7 games, 1.3 m_i50/g) **[data]** give the Tigers two contested-mark targets if the entries arrive cleanly. Ross is the most reliable senior midfielder in form (CV 0.18, **+0.77 trend**) **[data]**.

## St Kilda's edge

Disposal volume rank 8/18 (376.7/g), marks rank 5/18 (102.8/g), inside-50 marks rank 6/18 (13.44/g) **[data]**. Jack Sinclair is the season's quiet star - **29.4 disp/g (squad-high), 20.6 kicks/g, CV 0.14 (most consistent ball-winner in either squad)** **[data]**. Mitch Owens is a 1.67 g/g goalkicker (15 from 9) **[data]** with the inside-50 marking volume to match (1.8 m_i50/g) - the most efficient forward target either side fields. The Saints are 7-of-8 in their last eight against Richmond and have won the recent ones with structure: clean kicking from defence, Sinclair through the corridor, Marshall over Richmond's depleted ruck stocks. Their league-average pressure (51.3 tk/g, 15/18) is the only soft underbelly.

## The key matchup

**Patrick Taranto + Jacob Hopper vs Jack Sinclair + Sam Flanders - the clearance battle.** Sinclair and Flanders are St Kilda's volume midfield (29.4 + 24.0 = 53.4 disp/g combined) **[data]**. Taranto and Hopper are Richmond's contested-ball pair (12.9 + 11.1 = 24.0 CP/g combined) **[data]**. The Saints win on disposal volume; the Tigers can win at the stoppage. If Richmond's stoppage win rate is +5 clearances or better, the Tigers' inside-50 count gets to a survivable number (45+, where their forward 50 marking can score). If Sinclair is allowed to launch from the wing without pressure, the territory war is lost.

## Win condition (Richmond)

- **Inside-50 count within 3.** Richmond's i50 rank is 17/18 (47.8/g); St Kilda's is 12/18 (51.2/g) **[data]**. A 4+ entry gap is unwinnable at 0.193 goals/i50 (Richmond's 2026 conversion rate).
- **Hit-outs +5 or better.** Pick Ryan. Marshall ruck-rotation moments are where Richmond steals 2-3 stoppages.
- **First quarter within 6.** Richmond's average Q1 differential is **-10.9** **[data]**; St Kilda's is **+11.6** **[data]**. The 22-point swing is the league's biggest first-quarter gap. Survive the first 6 minutes and the game opens up.

## Win condition (St Kilda)

- **Sinclair to 27+ disposals.** When Sinclair hits that floor, he is launching from the wing and the Saints' transition chain is intact.
- **Owens 2+ goals.** The most efficient forward in either lineup. If Owens kicks straight, the score is comfortable.
- **Tackle count over 56.** Saints are 15/18 on tackles (51.3/g); they are league average pressure side. A 56+ tackle game forces Richmond's worst-clanger profile (59.0/g, 15/18) **[data]** into open errors.
