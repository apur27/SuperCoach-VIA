Source: 2026 player files **[data]**.

**Read**: Two distributors out of half-back, almost identical CV (both ~0.15 = metronomic). Short kicks more (19.2 vs 15.8) - this is the largest measurable difference. Hill is the Milera-replacement on the wing if the listed-out tag holds, and his kicking-mark profile is the closest fit to what Milera does. Whichever distributor controls the back half wins the territory war.

**Plan**: Short is Richmond's offensive launcher - free him from defensive responsibility, use him as the +1 across half-back. Hill in his new wing role is less familiar with the angled centre-bounce sweep that Milera owns; force him into a defensive role by running a small forward (Rioli or Green) at his receive.

**Trigger to abandon**: If Hill has 18+ disposals by half-time and 12+ are kicks, the wing-vacated-by-Milera plan has not worked and a hard tag (Brown Tom or Sonsie) is needed.

Source: 2026 player files **[data]**.

**Read**: This is the single most important matchup in the game. Sinclair is the most consistent ball-winner in either squad (29.4 disp/g, CV 0.14, 20.6 kicks/g - all top in either lineup). Flanders is the second-tier midfielder with 24 disp/g and the only Saints midfielder above 3 tackles/g. Taranto and Hopper are Richmond's contested-ball pair - they win at the stoppage but Sinclair wins in space.

**Plan**: Body-on-body at every stoppage. Taranto on Sinclair at the centre bounce (size match) - the goal is to remove Sinclair's first three kicks, not all of them. Hopper sits on Flanders inside; his CV 0.13 means he gives 22 disposals every week and the defensive role suits. The win isn't the disposal count; it's the *quality* of Sinclair's first kick. Force the handball, not the lobbed pass.

**Trigger to abandon**: If Sinclair has 18+ disposals at half-time with 10+ kicks, swap the assignment - Taranto to Sinclair full-time, Hopper to the next inside mid, and Ross slides to Flanders.

Source: 2026 player files **[data]**.

**Read**: Lynch is Richmond's tallest contested target inside 50 (2.6 marks-inside-50 per game) and is in form (+1.40 trend, 1.40 g/g). Wilkie is the Saints' premier intercept defender - **11 marks per game is the squad high by 4 marks** - and is the player Richmond's long entries land on if the kick isn't perfect. Wilkie's disposal trend is sharply negative (-1.63), which usually signals either fatigue or a deliberate role narrowing toward intercept-only.

**Plan**: Lead-up target on every entry. Front of the pack. Force Wilkie to defend on the lead, not on the long bomb where his intercept rate is highest. Lynch's contested-mark threat is real (4.2 marks/g, 6.2 CP/g) and Wilkie's tackle count (2.1/g) means once Lynch marks on the lead, he gets a clean shot. The plan is: short entries on the lead, never long bombs to a stationary contest.

**Trigger to abandon**: If Lynch is 0/3 in marking contests by half-time, swap him to a deeper role and rotate Lalor as the lead-up target.

Don't let Marshall get clean possession out the back of a stoppage where Ryan can't compete on the ground.

If Owens drifts to half-forward, Brown Tom (13.0 disp/g, 4.0 marks/g) picks him up; Vlastuin stays as the goal-square sentinel.

- CV is meaningful only above ~12 disp/g and 5+ games; small samples are flagged.
- **No injury or availability check applied**. Milera's absence is the assumed input per the executive summary; all other names assumed available. The team list is the senior coach's call.
- Position labels are inferred from stat profile, not authoritative.
