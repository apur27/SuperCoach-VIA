## Tactical blueprint - five plans

The brief is to steal a game from the structurally better team. Every plan below has a stat trigger and an abandonment clause.

### Plan 1: Contested ball - win at the stoppage, not in the open

The Saints win the volume game (376.7 disp/g vs Richmond 330.7) **[data]**. Richmond cannot win an open footy game. The only winnable battle is the stoppage.

**What we do**: Plant Taranto on Sinclair at every centre bounce. Hopper on Flanders inside. Ross as the third inside mid with permission to roam. Prestia as a wing-forward to rotate into the ruck divide. Don't ask Lalor or McAuliffe to win contested ball against Sinclair/Flanders - they are forward-mid hybrids, not engine-room bulls.

**Stat trigger**: Clearance count +5 or better at half time. If clearances are level or below, the stoppage plan is failing and we have to accept the open-play loss.

**Abandonment**: If Sinclair has 18+ disposals with 10+ kicks at half time, Taranto's role narrows to stoppage-only and Ross moves onto Sinclair full-time.

### Plan 2: Forward entries - short to the lead, never to the contest

Richmond's m_i50 per i50 is 0.219 (17/18) **[data]**. Wilkie averages 11 marks/g (squad-high for the Saints) and most of those come from spoiled or intercepted long bombs.

**What we do**: Every inside-50 entry is a short, low kick to a leading target. Lynch leads up; Lalor or Campbell as the second target. No long bombs to a stationary contest in the goal square. If the kick can't be short to a lead, retain in the corridor with handball.

**Stat trigger**: Marks inside 50 = 8 by half-time. If we're below 6, the entry plan is failing and we go to ground-ball pressure (Green / Lefau / Rioli) at the kick-in.

**Abandonment**: Same trigger as above. If we hit 8+ m_i50 by half-time, the plan is working; push it to 14+ for the game.

### Plan 3: Defensive setup - body on Owens, deny the lead

Owens is the Saints' goal-kicking weapon (15 goals from 9, 1.67/g, 1.8 m_i50/g) **[data]**. Vlastuin in front on every entry. Brown Tom as the secondary defender. Balta floats as the third tall.

**What we do**: Owens does not beat a defender in the air - he beats him to the spot. Vlastuin takes the spot. If Owens drifts to half-forward, Brown picks him up. The other Saints forwards (Wood, Phillipou, Sharman) get standard mid-defender matchups.

**Stat trigger**: Owens at 1 goal or fewer at half-time. If he has 2+, the defensive plan is failing and the second defender drops on him.

**Abandonment**: If Owens has 3 goals at any point, triple-team him at every kick - even at the cost of conceding the second-tall (Wood) free.

### Plan 4: Stoppages - Ryan first 25 minutes of every quarter

The hit-out battle is winnable if Ryan plays (16.3 HO/g across 3 games) **[data]**. Marshall is in better all-round form but is a single-body ruck - he tires.

**What we do**: Ryan on at every centre bounce for the first 25 minutes of each quarter. Nankervis (2 games, 20.0 HO/g) **[data]** as the second ruck for around-the-ground stoppages. Balta (13.3 HO/g across 8 games) **[data]** is the emergency third option if Ryan is hurt early. Don't try to outwork Marshall around the ground - try to win at the centre bounce only.

**Stat trigger**: Hit-out count +5 or better at half-time. Combined with a +3 centre-bounce-clearance count, the stoppage plan is delivering.

**Abandonment**: If Ryan's hit-out count is under 8 at half-time, swap to a 50/50 Ryan/Nankervis split for the second half. If both are below 10, the ruck contest is lost - accept and double-down on the open-ground tackle pressure.

### Plan 5: Set pieces - kick-ins to the corridor only with a target

Richmond's kick-in turnover problem has been a 2026 theme (clangers 59.0/g, 15/18) **[data]**. Most kick-ins go to a contest that the Saints' marking unit (5/18 marks, 13.4 m_i50/g for them, but defensive marks too) intercepts.

**What we do**: Every kick-in goes short or to a designated corridor target. Short to Brown or Vlastuin (both kick well). Never long down the line to a contest - the Wilkie-led intercept defence is too strong.

**Stat trigger**: Kick-in retention rate >65% (Richmond's first-receiver retention). Tracked manually; if the Saints score 3+ goals from intercept-mark chains, the plan is failing.

**Abandonment**: If 3+ goals concede from kick-in turnovers by half-time, the plan changes to bowled-out long kicks - accept the contest at 50% odds rather than the structured short kick at 60% retention but 40% turnover-into-immediate-score.

## What to watch at quarter-time

The signals that the plan is working:

- **Hit-out count.** Richmond +3 or better at QT means Ryan is winning the centre bounces. Ryan's foot health is the prerequisite for the whole plan; first quarter is the test.
- **Stoppage clearances.** +2 or better. If we're winning the contest at the bounce, we're inside 50 more often than we deserve to be.
- **Sinclair disposals.** Under 8 disposals = Taranto tag is working. Over 12 = Sinclair is winning the kick clean and the second-half pressure plan needs to escalate.
- **Owens marks inside 50.** 0 = Vlastuin is between him and the goal. 1+ = the defensive plan needs Brown to drop on him at every contest in Q2.
- **Marks inside 50 (RIC).** 3+ in Q1 = the short-kick entry plan is being executed. 0-1 = back to long bombs and Wilkie is collecting them.

If three or more of these five signals are in the green at quarter time, the plan is delivering and we just need to hold the structure for 80 more minutes. If three or more are red, the matchup is unwinnable on the original plan and the half-time message is to compress the game - keep the contest tight, accept a 12-15 point loss, and don't allow the 2025 R4 (-82) blowout to repeat.
