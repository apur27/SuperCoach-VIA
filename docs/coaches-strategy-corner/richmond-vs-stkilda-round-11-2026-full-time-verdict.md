- **Pre-match tracking must extend to the second-tier midfielder.** Macrae's line (31 and 7) was the game. He was not in the named match-ups. The lesson for the Match-up Tactician lens: when modelling an opposition midfield, predict the top five names by season-rate disposals, not the top three. A tackling midfielder who is also a high-handball-receive distributor is the modern hybrid that wins centre bounces and chase-down chains in the same shift.
- **Ruck dominance in hit-outs translates one-for-one to first-use and territory.** 42-19 is not a stat to note in the brief and move on from - it is the brief. When the ruck differential is forecast to be +20 or worse, the structural lever is downstream of every other plan, and the recommendation tier has to be capped accordingly. Richmond's brief tier-capped on player availability but did not tier-cap on the ruck mismatch, and should have.

- **"X kicked a goal off un-tagged entries" is the standing rebuttal to single-target tagging.** Hall got tagged from Q2 and St Kilda produced damage from Wilson and Phillipou instead. The Match-up Tactician lens needs to ask not "who do we tag" but "if we tag X, who is their second-and-third source of inside-50 damage" - and if there are three credible answers, a tag is wasted personnel.
- **Hill's role was misread.** The brief assumed Milera's absence would push Hill into an attacking-launcher role; the reality is St Kilda's defensive setup with Milera out is more conservative, not more attacking, and the launch responsibility shifted into the midfield. Next pre-match brief against this opposition with this personnel mix should reverse the prior on Hill's role.
