## Executive summary

Three things win this game.

1. **Make them play in Melbourne.** Adelaide are a 62.4% team at home and a 39.4% team on the road across 433 home and 378 away games since 1991 **[data]**. At the MCG specifically they sit at 32W–50L–1D — a 38.6% win rate across 83 games and just 30% from their last 10 **[data]**. They have not yet adapted their style to neutral-venue football. Travel + venue is worth a leg up before the bounce.
2. **Win the contested-ball war by accepting their volume and beating their pressure.** Adelaide rank 4th of 18 for tackles (59.5/g) and 7th for contested possessions (130.1/g) **[data]**. They will hunt; they will sting. The counter is *clean handball release under pressure* — not playing keepings-off, but moving the ball one tackle ahead. Richmond's contested-ball share (CP/disposal = 0.371) already exceeds the league average of 0.350 **[data]**. The win-able battle is at the second-receiver, not the first contest.
3. **Attack the Adelaide ruck and the Adelaide forward arc.** Adelaide are 18th of 18 for hit-outs (8.8/g — a stunning 24-hit-out gap to the league average of 32.8) **[data]**. They are running a no-recognised-ruck split between Riley Thilthorpe (3.3 HO/g) and Finnbar Maley (7.0 HO/g). Samson Ryan averaged 16.3 HO/g in his three games this year **[data]**. If Ryan plays, this is a structural mismatch worth 5–8 stoppage clearances. Forward of centre, Adelaide concede inside-50 marks at the 14th rate and trail 13.3 league-avg goals/g (Adelaide 12.5; Richmond 9.2) **[data]** — but their territorial defence is real (rebound-50s rank 2nd of 18). The lever is *quality of entry*, not volume.

The most recent tape Adelaide will be watching is the Round 8 Brisbane game at the Gabba: 22 goals 11 (127), Adelaide held to 11.9 (75), CPs 111 conceded **[data]**. That game showed Adelaide's interstate pressure floor. The upcoming MCG fixture is the second leg of that travel block.

## Tactical blueprint — how Richmond wins

### 1. Contested ball strategy

**The number**: Adelaide CPs 130.1/g (7/18), Richmond CPs 122.1/g (15/18). Adelaide tackle rate 59.5 (4/18), Richmond 51.8 (16/18) **[data]**.

**Read**: A pure ground-ball war, Richmond loses by ~10 a game. The most recent West Coast win proved it can be flipped (141 CPs, season high) but only because Richmond went man-on-man at the contest and accepted faster ball movement.

**The plan**:
- Match Sam Berry with a senior body — Jack Ross is the natural fit (5.1 tk/g, 11.9 CP/g, +0.83 trend) **[data]**.
- Run 3 mids through the centre square: Ross, Taranto, Prestia. Hopper as wing/release.
- At secondary stoppages (forward and defensive 50) prioritise *handball release* over winning the ball cleanly. Adelaide's tackle rate punishes possession-holders, not handball-receivers.
- Target Adelaide's CP weakness in transition — Richmond sits 6/18 for contested marks (9.4/g) **[data]**. If you cannot win the ground ball, win the air *behind* the contest with Balta or Lynch.

**Lever**: If Samson Ryan plays, he wins the ruck (16.3 HO/g vs Adelaide's combined 8.8). That single mismatch is worth 5–8 stoppage clearances over a game.

### 2. Forward entry and scoring

**The number**: Richmond goals/i50 = 0.192. Adelaide goals/i50 = 0.253. League average 0.249 **[data]**. Adelaide rebound-50s rank 2/18 (41.8/g).

**Read**: Long bombs come back. Richmond cannot win this game by volume (they don't have it) or by speed (Adelaide rebounds it). They have to hit a target each entry.

**The plan**:
- Entries to a contest, not space. Richmond's contested marks rank (6/18) is the asset.
- Identify Tom Lynch (3.7 m_i50/g, 4 goals from 4 games = 1.00 g/g) and Liam Fawcett (3.0 m_i50/g, 5 goals from 3 games = 1.67 g/g) as the contest targets. Noah Balta (2.5 m_i50/g, 4 goals from 7 games = 0.57 g/g) is the third option **[data]**.
- Seth Campbell (11 goals from 8 games = 1.38 g/g, squad-high total) is a small-forward finisher — needs ground-ball delivery, not high balls.
- Avoid the Adelaide rebound trigger: Worrell intercept marks at 6.4/g, Milera distributes from there. Instead of a long kick to the pocket, use short kicks from half-forward to retain possession and reset.
- **Target conversion lift from 0.192 to 0.230 = +1.8 goals = +11 points.**

### 3. Defensive setup against Adelaide's key forwards

**The number**: Walker 1.57 g/g (11 total, max 5), Rachele 1.50 g/g (12 total, max 4), Thilthorpe 1.62 g/g (13 total, max 3), Keays 1.25 g/g (10 total, max 3) **[data]**. Walker max-game 5 goals; if he gets 5 the game is gone.

**The plan**:
- **Walker** gets the senior intercept defender. Nick Vlastuin (4.3 m/g, 14.3 d/g, +0.54 trend) is the body for the contest **[data]**. Walker is veteran but slow — front-and-square.
- **Thilthorpe** is the dual-role threat (forward + ruck). When he goes ruck, his replacement is mostly Maley — that is the moment to exit defensive 50 quickly. When he is forward, he is the contested-mark target — Tom Brown or a tall pinch-hitter.
- **Rachele/Rankine** are mobile forwards. They run into space. Match them with running defenders, not stoppers.
- **Keays** is the pressure-and-snap forward. Tackler-finder. Don't give the smalls clean front-foot ball at half-forward.
- Adelaide's marks inside 50 rank is 14/18 (11.0/g). If Richmond hold their inside-50 mark concession to 10 or fewer, the score should sit under 90.

### 4. Stoppages — clearance battle plan

**The number**: Adelaide hit-outs 8.8/g (18/18). Richmond hit-outs 23.4 (17/18). Samson Ryan when selected: 16.3/g **[data]**. League avg 32.8.

**Read**: The single biggest structural lever in the game. Adelaide are running a no-recognised-ruck rotation between Thilthorpe and Maley. Any side with a competent ruckman wins the hit-out battle by 15+. Richmond's third stat in this brief is hit-outs. **You don't get this opportunity twice a year.**

**The plan**:
- Pick Ryan if fit. Period.
- Centre bounces: load the engine room. Ross + Taranto + Prestia at the bounce, Lalor at half-forward as the receive. **Berry will go to the contest; Dawson will be at clearance; Peatling will tackle.** Ross outlasts Berry on stamina; Taranto outlasts Dawson at the contest.
- Throw-ins: forward-50 throw-ins give Richmond the score; defensive-50 throw-ins are where Adelaide hurt teams (Keays, Rachele finish). Risk-manage by getting it out of bounds rather than a low-percentage clearing kick.
- Around-the-ground stoppages: the priority is to *not let the third Adelaide stoppage become a clearance via Berry* — he wins 13.4 CPs/g; he is the threat.

### 5. Set pieces — attacking and defensive

**The number**: Goal-kicking accuracy 2026 — Richmond 48.4%, Adelaide 54.1% **[data]**.

**Read**: Adelaide are a more accurate set-shot side. Richmond cannot afford behinds in a tight game.

**The plan**:
- **Kick-ins**: Adelaide's rebound-50 rank (2/18) is partly built on transitioning kick-ins. Richmond mark inside 50 only 10.9/g (15/18) — meaning Adelaide will get a kick-in around 7–10 times per game. Set the kick-in zone deep, force the long kick, intercept with Vlastuin or Broad in the corridor.
- **Stoppage forward 50s**: Throw the numbers in. Richmond won 13 of 24 stoppages within forward 50 across the recent West Coast win — score-from-stoppage was their highest-yielding chain.
- **Set shots from outside 50**: Richmond's accuracy says no. Take 30m shots; pass on 50m+ unless they are dead centre.
- **Boundary throw-ins inside attacking 50**: Adelaide's tap-ruck rotation cannot match Ryan. This is the single highest-leverage set play of the day.

## Pre-game checklist (10 tactical points)

1. **Win the bounce, win the first 6 minutes.** Richmond Q1 differential is –12.2/g; correct from the first centre bounce.
2. **Pick Ryan if available.** Adelaide hit-outs are 18/18 (8.8/g). This is an opportunity that does not recur.
3. **Match Berry with Ross.** Berry leads Adelaide on contested poss (13.4/g) AND tackles (8.0/g) — he is the single point of failure in their pressure system.
4. **Vlastuin on Walker.** The veteran key forward needs the body. Front-and-square; do not let him get the contested mark in front.
5. **Targets for inside-50s**: Lynch / Fawcett / Balta in the air; Campbell on the ground. Not long bombs to space.
6. **Avoid kick-ins long down the line**. Adelaide are 2/18 at rebound-50s (41.8/g) — the line kick is their food.
7. **Cap clangers under 55**. Richmond average 59.2 (4/18 worst). Adelaide score 0.253 goals per inside 50 — every defensive turnover costs 1.5 points.
8. **Forward 50 boundary throw-ins are the set play of the day.** Ryan + Lalor + Lynch as the loaded structure.
9. **Don't chase score with style.** If the game is close at 3QT, hold structure — Adelaide's average Q4 score is 20.8/g vs opposition 23.9/g (–3.1 per Q4) **[data]**. They tighten in close games.
10. **Stay on the ball, off the umpires.** Adelaide rank 6/18 for one-percenters (45.8/g) — they will compete behind the play. Don't get drawn into off-ball fights; they win those.

## Caveats & methodology notes

- **Data window**: 2026 form is the eight completed games per club at the time of writing. All numbers stable on a per-game basis but the sample is small enough that one outlier (e.g. Adelaide's 167-CP game vs Carlton, or Richmond's 280-disposal game vs North Melbourne) influences league rank by 1–2 places.
- **Pre-match status**: This brief is built for the upcoming Richmond–Adelaide fixture at the M.C.G. on 10 May 2026, 3:15pm. The match has not yet been played. The two clubs did not meet in the most recently completed round, so the form numbers carry over directly into the pre-match read. Anything stated about the match itself (predicted matchups, recommended structures, expected pressure points) is forward-looking; only the form, identity, and head-to-head numbers are grounded in completed results.
- **Position data is not in the dataset**. Player roles (mid/forward/back) are inferred from stat profiles. A player with high marks_inside_50 and goals is treated as a forward; a player with high tackles, contested possessions and clearances is a midfielder. This is honest inference, not authoritative position.
