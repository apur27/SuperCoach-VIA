## What this measures

A hit-out is a ruck contest in which a player taps or palms the ball away from a bounce, throw-in, or ball-up. It is the most position-specific stat in football: only ruckmen accumulate them, and the metric measures one half of the ruck's job (the other being post-clearance work, which appears as disposals and clearances). Career hit-out volume is the closest approximation we have to a "career ruck dominance" number.

> **Data caveat.** The AFL changed how contested ruck taps are logged around **2017**. Pre-2017 totals tend to be slightly understated relative to post-2017 totals because the modern method counts tap-down attempts in a slightly broader way. Direct comparisons across the 2017 divide are meaningful at the rough-order-of-magnitude level only.

## FootyStrategy tactical read

**Goldstein's untouchable total.** Todd Goldstein 10,597 hit-outs **[data]** is the only career past 10,000. Gawn at 8,703 **[data]** is the closest active threat - 2,029 hit-outs behind, requiring approximately 60 more games at his current ~33/game rate to catch up. Realistically, this record stands for the foreseeable future. *Structuralist lens:* Goldstein's career at North Melbourne was the textbook one-ruckman team model - sole ruck, no rest rotations, every centre bounce. The single-ruckman pattern is now largely extinct; modern teams carry two rucks (Gawn + Tomlinson at Melbourne, Witts + Lobb at Gold Coast historically) and the workload spreads.

**Per-game rates jumped post-2017.** O'Brien 35.39 **[data]**, Witts 34.59 **[data]**, Gawn 33.22 **[data]**, Jacobs 32.63 **[data]**, Grundy 31.66 **[data]**, Sandilands 31.37 **[data]**, Goldstein 30.81 **[data]** - the 30+/game rates cluster heavily in the post-2017 ruckmen. *Tempo Architect lens:* the 2017 hit-out recording change combined with the post-2018 nominated-ruckman rule has pushed the per-game ceiling upward. Pre-2017 ruckmen at the same physical level (Cox, Justin Madden, Simon Madden, Jeff White) sit at 13-23 per game - genuinely lower output, but also genuinely lower-counted output. The exact split between rule effect and recording effect is impossible to disentangle from this data alone.

**The 1980s-90s ruck legends are understated.** Cox 22.86 **[data]**, Dempsey 19.69 **[data]**, Justin Madden 17.31 **[data]**, Simon Madden 13.83 **[data]**, Jeff White 18.66 **[data]**, Peter Everitt 17.05 **[data]** - the pre-2010 ruckmen on this list have per-game rates that look comically low compared to the modern era. *Match-up Tactician lens:* their actual ruck dominance was substantial - Cox was the foundation ruckman of West Coast's 2006 premiership, Dempsey won the 1975 Brownlow as a ruckman - but the recording methodology of their era credited fewer of their tap-downs as hit-outs. The 10-13 hit-out delta per game is largely an artefact, not a real performance gap.

**The career-vs-rate trade-off.** Gawn, Grundy, and O'Brien all have higher per-game rates than Goldstein but trail his career total because of injury or career-length differences. *Conditioner lens:* the ruckman's body bears the highest cumulative contact load of any position in football. Sandilands (211cm) needed three knee surgeries; Grundy has had multiple shoulder issues; Naitanui's knee injuries cost him 80+ games of prime career. The fact that Goldstein played 344 games **[data]** without a major absence is the single largest reason his record stands - not because his per-game rate was unmatched, but because the post-2010 game has produced no other 300+ game high-rate ruckman.

**The Naitanui exception.** Nic Naitanui 5,549 hit-outs **[data]** in only 213 games for 26.05/game **[data]** is the lowest sustained per-game rate of any "elite" modern ruckman - yet his impact on the game's centre-bounce work for a decade was disproportionate to that number. *Talent Developer lens:* Naitanui specialised in the highest-pressure, highest-value taps (centre bounces won cleanly to space) rather than the volume-maximising every-stoppage hit-out. His hit-out total badly understates his actual ruck-craft value. Hit-out efficiency (taps to advantage / total taps) is not in the database; if it were, Naitanui would likely lead the modern era.

## Data coverage

- **Hit-outs recorded league-wide from the early 1960s** for most teams, with full coverage from 1965 forward.
- **2017 recording change** broadened what counted as a tap. Pre-2017 totals understate relative to post-2017.
- **Hit-outs to advantage** (a more recent stat that measures taps directed to a teammate at speed) is not tracked in this database.

## Methodology

Sum of per-game `hit_outs` field across each player's career. Per-game rate is total ÷ games_played.
