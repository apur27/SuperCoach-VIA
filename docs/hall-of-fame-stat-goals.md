## What this measures

Goals are the simplest, oldest, most reliable stat in AFL/VFL history. Every team has counted them since 1897. Every game record carries one. Unlike disposals, tackles, or clearances - whose recording conventions have shifted decade by decade - goals are the only line on the ledger where a 1925 entry and a 2025 entry mean the same thing arithmetically. What has changed enormously is how easy it is to kick them.

## FootyStrategy tactical read

**What this list measures.** Goals are the only outcome that directly produces points. Everything else on every other stat page on this site - disposals, tackles, hit-outs, clearances - is an input that feeds, eventually, into someone's chance to kick one of these. A career goal tally is the most direct measure of a player's contribution to the only statistic that decides matches.

**The 1,000-goal club is closed.** Six players have ever cleared 1,000 career goals: Lockett **[data]** 1,360, Coventry **[data]** 1,299, Dunstall **[data]** 1,254, Franklin **[data]** 1,066, Wade **[data]** 1,057, Ablett snr **[data]** 1,031. Five of them played in the high-scoring eras between 1920-1995. Franklin is the lone post-2000 entrant, and the next closest active or recent forward - Hawkins at 796 **[data]** - finished 270 goals short. *Conditioner lens:* the modern interchange rule, the post-2014 reduction in rotations, and zone defence have produced a permanent compression in goals-per-team-per-game. The post-2000 forwards on this list (Franklin, Lloyd, Richardson, Hawkins, J. Riewoldt, Cameron, Rocca, Hall) average 2.65 goals per game; the pre-2000 forwards above them average 3.79. That is not a finishing skills gap. It is a structural difference in how many shots each forward gets per match.

**Per-game rate vs total volume - the era reframing.** Lockett 4.84/game **[data]**, Dunstall 4.66 **[data]**, McKenna 4.58 **[data]** and Ablett snr 4.16 **[data]** sit in a band that no post-2000 forward approaches. Franklin's 3.01 **[data]** is the highest sustained per-game rate of any 1,000-goal forward since the year 2000. *Structuralist lens:* prior to zone defence, full-forwards operated in one-on-one space with the goal-square as a fixed reference; modern key forwards push up, lead at angle to take marks 50 metres out, and operate inside a defensive structure that denies the easy direct lead. The shot-per-mark conversion is similar; the shots-per-game count is what has fallen. McKenna's 191 games for 874 goals **[data]** is a per-game rate that no modern player has matched over a similar span.

**One-club specialisation.** Coventry, Titus, Lloyd, Dunstall, Bartlett, Hawkins, Richardson, Riewoldt, Mohr, Kernahan - ten of the top twenty played for a single club. *Culture Custodian lens:* the centre-half-forward/full-forward archetype produces deep club identification; these players become the structural reference point a club's forward set-up is built around for a decade or more. Multi-club forwards (Lockett, Franklin, Wade, Ablett, Hall, Cameron, Rocca) are the exception, not the norm. The forward role's intricate timing with the rest of the team's spread, the kicking-in structure, and the contested-mark cohort behind the ball appears to reward continuity.

**Where the list under-reports.** Goals are the one category on this site that is reliable back to 1897 without major caveat. But playing-era effects compress the comparison the other way: the 22-game era (post-1968) gave players around 22 chances per season; earlier seasons of 18 games gave Coventry, Titus, Pratt and Mohr fewer opportunities even in the high-scoring 1920s-1930s. Pratt's 150-goal 1934 season in 18 home-and-away games is the most efficient goalkicking season ever recorded **[data]** (see [single-season records](hall-of-fame-stat-single-season.md)). The list above sorts on raw volume; the [era-normalised top 100](hall-of-fame-top100.md) handles the era-adjusted version.

## Data coverage

- **Goals are reliable back to 1897** for all VFL/AFL clubs. No major coverage gap.
- **Per-game rate** is sensitive to season length: 1920s-30s seasons were 18 home-and-away games, the modern era is 23.
- **Finals goals are included** in the per-game CSVs.
- Numbers verified from `data/player_data/<player>_performance_details.csv` via `docs/hall-of-fame/compute_stat_leaders.py`.

## Methodology

Sums per-game `goals` field from each player's career performance file. Top-20 list ranks by career total; ties are joint-ranked (e.g. "5="). Per-game rate is total ÷ games_played (the per-row running game counter), not row count - see `compute_stat_leaders.py` and the agent memory note on the games-counter gotcha.
