## What this measures

The Brownlow Medal is awarded annually to the fairest and best player in the home-and-away season, as judged by the three field umpires after every match (3, 2, 1 votes per game). The career vote total is not a measure of "best player" but of "most consistently impressive to the umpires across many seasons". The two questions overlap heavily but are not identical - the umpire judgement systematically favours midfielders who win contested possessions and clearances, and systematically discounts forwards (who control the game in fewer touches) and defenders (whose work is preventative).

> **Data caveat.** The database stores votes on the modern **3-2-1 scale** (in use since 1981). Pre-1981 the scale was 5-4-3-2-1; those totals have been rescaled for comparability. Headline career figures from AFL.com.au will sometimes look larger than the numbers here because they pre-rescaling.

## FootyStrategy tactical read

**What the Brownlow actually measures.** The vote profile rewards three things: (1) the player who is on the ball at centre bounces, (2) the player whose disposals visibly hit a target inside-50, and (3) the player whose game has 30+ possessions and 7+ tackles. *Match-up Tactician lens:* this is a contested-midfielder template. Of the top 20 above, exactly two are not midfielders (Franklin, the rare forward exception; Goodes, who was a half-forward in his best vote years). Every other name was a centre-bounce or wing-rotation midfielder in their peak vote-getting seasons.

**Ablett jnr's 262 is the all-time mark.** Gary Ablett jnr 262 votes **[data]** across 357 games (0.73/game **[data]**) won two Brownlows (2009, 2013), was runner-up another two times, and polled votes in 70-80% of his games. *Structuralist lens:* his combination of contested ball-winning and post-clearance burst was the umpire-favoured archetype made flesh. Dangerfield 259 **[data]** is on track to overhaul Ablett's mark if he plays two more full seasons - he requires another 12 votes, achievable in a single strong year.

**Per-game vote rate is the elite-density metric.** Cripps 0.77/game **[data]**, Fyfe 0.77 **[data]**, Judd 0.75 **[data]**, Ablett 0.73 **[data]**, Swan 0.72 **[data]**, Martin 0.71 **[data]** and Bontempelli 0.70 **[data]** are the seven careers above 0.70 votes/game. *Tempo Architect lens:* a 0.70 rate corresponds to roughly one vote every game and a half, sustained across an entire career. Only a small number of midfielders ever produce this density because the umpire vote distribution is highly concentrated - 3 votes go to one player per game, and votes are biased toward the winning team and toward midfielders with multiple "moments" inside the 50.

**Pendlebury 225 votes in 435 games (0.52/game).** Pendlebury's per-game rate **[data]** is the lowest of any top-10 player on this list - he sits #4 by accumulating votes across the longest career in modern AFL history. *Culture Custodian lens:* he polled less densely than Ablett or Dangerfield because his game involved less spectacular ball-winning and more distribution-driven impact - the kind of game that wins matches but does not produce highlight-reel umpire-impression moments. The fact that he is the only player to clear 200 career votes in fewer than 5+ votes per season in his peak years is itself a statement about what the umpire vote does and does not reward.

**The all-time-medallist absence.** Several three-time or four-time Brownlow Medallists are not on this list at all. Haydn Bunton snr (3 medals, 1931-35), Dick Reynolds (3 medals, 1934-38), Bob Skilton (3 medals, 1959-68) sit below the modern leaders because (1) the pre-1981 5-4-3-2-1 rescaling brings their totals down, and (2) their careers were shorter than the modern 250-game minimum needed for top-20 entry. *Talent Developer lens:* the leaderboard above is a longevity-times-vote-rate list, not a pure "umpires liked them best" list. A 4-Brownlow career compressed into 180 games would sit lower on this page than a 1-Brownlow career stretched to 380 games at moderate vote rate.

**The 1990s undervote.** Robert Harvey 215 votes **[data]** in 383 games (0.56/game) **[data]** was a two-time Brownlow Medallist (1997, 1998); his per-game rate sits below the modern elite because the 1990s game was less centre-bounce-dominant than today's. *Tempo Architect lens:* the post-2014 rule changes that produced more stoppages per game also produced more high-contested-possession matches, which produce more high-vote midfielders. The vote density of the post-2010 era is structurally higher than the 1990s for the same level of midfield dominance. Compare Harvey's 215 in 383 games to Cripps's 189 in 244 games **[data]** - very similar quality of dominance, very different per-game vote yield.

## Data coverage

- **Votes stored on the modern 3-2-1 scale.** Pre-1981 5-4-3-2-1 votes have been rescaled for comparability.
- **Ineligible-but-polled votes are included** in the career total (e.g. a player suspended in the season counts their votes here even if they did not "win" the medal).
- **Finals do not award Brownlow votes** - all numbers here are home-and-away only.

## Methodology

Sum of per-game `brownlow_votes` field across each player's career. The compute script normalises any pre-1981 vote scaling. Per-game rate uses home-and-away games only when the source CSV has finals flagged; otherwise total games are used.
