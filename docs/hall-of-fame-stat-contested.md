## What this measures

A contested possession is a ball-getting event won under direct opposition pressure: hard-ball get, loose-ball get under pressure, contested mark, free kick won. The metric isolates ball-winning that produces possession from a 50/50 (or worse) contest. Skill at this stat is not predictive of skill at the rest of the game - it is its own competency, the one that separates a "stoppage midfielder" from a "spread midfielder".

> **Data caveat - prominent.** Contested possessions only appear in the database from **1998**. This is fundamentally a post-2000 leaderboard. Every player on the list played the bulk of their career after the stat was tracked. Pre-1998 ball-getters - Greg Williams, Mark Mercuri, Robert DiPierdomenico, Wayne Carey, Tim Watson, Leigh Matthews's centre-square era - are not on this list because the numbers do not exist.

## FootyStrategy tactical read

**Dangerfield's outlier total.** Dangerfield 4,704 contested possessions **[data]** at 12.58/game over 370 games is an extreme combination of rate and longevity. The next closest are Pendlebury 4,412 **[data]** (volume-driven, 10.12/game) and Kennedy 4,007 **[data]** (rate-driven at 13.82/game over 290 games). *Structuralist lens:* Dangerfield's role across his Adelaide and Geelong careers has been the centre-bounce attack midfielder - first to the ball, body over the ground, with the size to win the contest and the speed to escape it. The role expects 12+ contested possessions per game; very few players have ever sustained it for 18 seasons without injury slowing the contact load.

**The per-game rate elite.** Oliver 14.76/game **[data]**, Cripps 14.52 **[data]**, Kennedy 13.82 **[data]**, Priddis 13.23 **[data]**, Neale 13.08 **[data]**, Fyfe 12.97 **[data]**, Dangerfield 12.68 **[data]**, Liberatore 12.22 **[data]** - the rate threshold for elite contested-ball winning is approximately 12.5/game across a full career. *Tempo Architect lens:* this corresponds to roughly one contested possession every two-and-a-bit minutes of in-play time, almost double the league-average midfielder rate. Cripps and Oliver are the active leaders by rate; if their bodies hold, both will overhaul Dangerfield's career total within five years.

**The two roles - bull and bullseye.** The list contains two distinct contested-possession archetypes. Cripps, Kennedy, Liberatore, Priddis, Neale are the "bull" - direct-contact ball-winners who put their body in the most likely place and physically dominate. Pendlebury, Sam Mitchell, Black, Boak are the "bullseye" - clean ball-handlers who find space at the back of the contest and convert ground-ball into clearance via skill, not force. *Match-up Tactician lens:* the bulls produce higher per-game rates over shorter careers (the body cannot sustain the contact load); the bullseyes produce lower per-game rates over longer careers. Both archetypes win games; the second produces 400-game careers.

**The pre-1998 silence is the biggest absence on this site.** Greg Williams's 1986 Norm Smith Medal performance was contested-ball football at its peak. Wayne Carey's 1996 hub work at centre half-forward, Tim Watson's centre-square rule, Leigh Matthews's 1979 hard-ball-get masterclass - none of these careers register on this list because contested possessions were not tracked. *Talent Developer lens:* this list shows the *traceable* post-2000 generation. If the project's database were retroactively coded back to 1965 (which is not feasible from public records), the leaderboard would shift substantially. As stated, this is a leaderboard of "what we can measure", not a "best ever" claim.

**Inside-50s carry the same caveat.** The companion metric inside-50s - the count of times a player carries the ball into the attacking 50-metre arc - is also dominated by post-2000 midfielders: Dangerfield 1,788 **[data]**, B. Harvey 1,639 **[data]**, Ablett jnr 1,620 **[data]**, Pendlebury 1,551 **[data]**, Boak 1,540 **[data]**. The same five players appear at the top of both lists because the same role (centre-bounce midfielder, possession-getter, kicker-inside) produces both stats. *Tempo Architect lens:* contested-possessions and inside-50s are the two stats that most cleanly identify "who attacked centre-bounce stoppages and got the ball going forward". They are highly correlated by design.

## Methodology
