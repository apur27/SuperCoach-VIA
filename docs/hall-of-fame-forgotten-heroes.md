## 1. The Quiet Workhorses
*150+ games, 20+ disposal average, never the headline. They ran all day and nobody wrote songs about it.*

### Andrew Gaff (2011-2024, West Coast)

The wing was a position other players passed through; for Gaff it was a fourteen-year occupation.

[data]: 280 games, 84 career goals, 25.7 disposal average **[data]** — sustained volume from the outside, not the contest. His 2019 peak of 31.9 disposals a game **[data]** is elite ball-winning by any standard.

The wing demands the rarest aerobic profile on the ground: cover the full width, push to both arcs, present as an option every transition and never stop. Gaff turned defensive 50 exits into uncontested chains by simply always being on the loose side of his man. His 25.7 disposal average **[data]**, almost entirely from the outside, is the statistical signature of that role — sustained, uncontested volume held across fourteen seasons on the wing.

The verdict: a metronomic wide-zone runner whose value lived in repeat efforts the box score rounds down to a possession.

### Matthew Boyd (2003-2017, Western Bulldogs)

He started as an undersized contested midfielder and finished as a rebounding half-back, and was elite at both.

[data]: 292 games, 86 career goals, 25.0 disposal average **[data]** — high volume across fifteen seasons. His 2012 season of 32.8 disposals a game **[data]** sits among the best ball-winning years of its era.

He was the safe outlet the whole structure leaned on.

The verdict: a dual-role craftsman who held two positions to an elite standard a decade apart.

The verdict: a two-way midfielder whose disposal quality let his team carry a heavier defensive load elsewhere.
