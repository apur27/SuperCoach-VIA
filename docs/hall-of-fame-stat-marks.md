## What this measures

Marks are clean possessions taken from a kick that travels 15+ metres. The mark is the single most efficient way to advance the football: it stops the clock, denies the opposition pressure, and creates the platform for an uncontested next disposal. The career marks leaderboard rewards two distinct archetypes - the leading key forward, and the contested-marking ruckman/defender - that take the ball out of dispute repeatedly across a long career.

## FootyStrategy tactical read

**Two archetypes dominate.** The top of the list splits cleanly into key forwards (Riewoldt, Loewe, Richardson, Salmon, Hawkins, Franklin, Hall) and rucks-or-tall-defenders (Dempsey, Madden, Roos, Goodes, Cloke). Riewoldt 2,944 marks **[data]** sits 38 ahead of Dempsey's 2,906 **[data]** - and Dempsey was primarily a ruckman whose per-game rate of 8.83 marks/game **[data]** is actually the highest in the top 20. *Match-up Tactician lens:* the marking position is the only position in football that produces 8+ marks per game over a career. Riewoldt's lead-up game and Dempsey's third-man-up ruck contest were both built around being the first body to the ball; everything else followed from that.

**The St Kilda concentration.** Three of the top four (Riewoldt, Loewe, and a tier down at Pavlich, Goddard) had their primary careers at St Kilda or Fremantle - clubs whose game plans repeatedly relied on a structured forward-line marking target as the main offensive avenue. *Structuralist lens:* Riewoldt's St Kilda was built around his lead patterns - direct kicks from defensive 50 to a single deep target, then re-direct from his mark. Loewe was the previous generation's version of the same template. When the entire team structure points at a single marker, the career mark count compounds.

**The Brad Johnson exception.** Johnson at 2,153 marks in 364 games **[data]** for 5.91/game is the only true wingman in the top eight. *Talent Developer lens:* his career arc - 364 games on a half-forward flank, regularly taking 6-8 marks against opposition mediums - shows the marking total is not solely a tall-player metric. Johnson's running pattern produced the ball more often because of where he positioned, not because he out-jumped tall opponents.

**Per-game rate vs total volume.** Dempsey 8.83 **[data]**, Riewoldt 8.76 **[data]**, Richardson 8.05 **[data]**, Loewe 7.80 **[data]** are the only four 7.5+/game marks-per-game careers in the top 20. Everyone else sits under 7. *Conditioner lens:* the elite key-forward template requires hard-running aerobic capacity to keep leading through three quarters; this is what separates Riewoldt and Richardson from the next tier. The Goddard/Shaw types at 6+/game are the elite intercept-marking defenders whose stat line is identical in shape but tactically inverted (they take marks defending, not attacking).

**Era effects.** Marks-per-game rates appear to be rising slightly in the modern era because of the post-2014 rules limiting contact at the marking contest. But none of the top-rate marks-per-game players above 8 are post-2000 by primary era. *Tempo Architect lens:* the modern game spreads marks across more players (the chip-to-chip mark count has risen, while the contested-mark count has fallen). A 2026 wingman might take 8+ marks/game but in a season-by-season check the figure rarely sustains over a 10-year span. Riewoldt and Dempsey's sustained dominance is unlikely to be challenged because the modern game does not concentrate marks in any single target the way 1970s-2000s football did.

## Data coverage

- **Marks were recorded from very early in VFL history** but kick-mark distinction (the 15-metre rule) was formalised in 1965; pre-1965 totals are not directly comparable.
- **Pre-1980 player files** are reliable for marks but mark-type splits (contested vs uncontested) are not separately stored.
- **The list omits 1900s-50s greats** (Coleman, Pratt, Whitten) because the per-game CSV coverage for their careers is patchy on marks.

## Methodology

Sum of per-game `marks` field across each player's career. Per-game rate is total ÷ games_played.
